About CryptoCLASS

Cryptography (n)

The science of coding and decoding messages to keep those messages secure

CryptoCLASS is designed for all abilities.

We aim to introduce easy-to-use programs to your everyday life that you can use to privately communicate online, store documents on a computer or external hard drive and browse anonymously.

We pride ourselves on being able to unpack tech-speak and jargon so, even if you have a different expertise, you will be able to leave with knowledge of how to download and use these programs to protect yourself.

CryptoCLASS is free to attend and is run by volunteers.

Values

Open-mindedness

we’re all very accepting and respectful of who you are, your profession and your ability

No one left behind

if you don’t get it, speak up and we’ll explain it in a different way. If you still don’t get it, we’ll try another way until everyone understands

Driven by risk-profiling

in other words, if your risk profile means you want to store documents privately, we’ll teach that. If you want to browse online anonymously, we’ll teach that.

This isn’t political, it’s educational

Technology

We will teach you to use a number of tools including:

TrueCrypt

TrueCrypt lets you encrypt your hard drive, thumb drive or files on your dropbox

Bitmessage

BitMessage is email for the truly security conscious

TOR

Securely and anonymously browse the internet, but be careful when using the onion router

CryptoCat

The easiest way to chat securely from Firefox or Chrome, CryptoCat uses the 'off the record protocol

TextSecure

The best way to secure your short messages, TextSecure is like WhatsApp, Hangouts or iMessage but doesnt leak your private text messages

The Fine Print

CryptoCLASS is a free non-political class run by @simonvc and @saronimo for the public good.

Attendance is free, bring something to drink.

The class runs from 19:30 (arrive at 19:00) until 21:00, followed by informal optional drinks at a nearby local bar

Classes 101, 102, 103 will deal with simple software, usually two of: Truecrypt, TextSecure, TOR, Bitmessage and CryptoCat

Classes 201, 202, 203 will deal with more complicated topics including PGP, OpenSSL and public/private keys.

Classes 3xx, will deal with advanced topics like whole disk encryption, TOR hidden services, Bitcoin, truly paranoid computing
